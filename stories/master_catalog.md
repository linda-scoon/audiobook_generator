## SERIES OVERVIEW

Ten standalone romance novels. Each is 200 chapters. Each follows a "he falls first, falls fast, falls hard" trope — the male lead is gone the moment he sees her; the female lead is slow to reciprocate. Each chapter targets 300–450 words for audiobook narration at 2–3 minutes per chapter.

The stories range across werewolf romance (modern and medieval), vampire noir, fae fantasy, dragon shifter, contemporary sports romance, mafia, music industry, and medical drama. They share trope DNA but no characters, no world, no continuity.

---

## RULES THAT APPLY TO ALL TEN STORIES

1. **NO REPETITION** of phrasing, descriptions, or beats within a story.
2. **HE FALLS FIRST, FALLS FAST, FALLS HARD.** The asymmetry is the engine.
3. **THE MEN ARE NOT SCRUBS.** Wealthy, powerful, independent. They do not live with their mothers.
4. **Closed-door / fade-to-black sex.** Maximum heat on the page up to the act itself; the act fades. After: marks, memory, references. (Story 4 is the exception — the conception scene is the inciting incident and must be vivid in fade-to-black terms.)

## STORY 1 — *The Alpha of Ashbrook*

**Logline:** A dystopian-world Alpha senator-equivalent recognizes his fated mate at a public hearing and takes her on the spot — but she is human, she does not feel the bond, and she will never forgive him for what he did in that room.

**Blurb:** Alpha Roman Vance has run the Vance pack territory for twelve years and has stopped expecting his mate. Elena Cruz has run a community gym on Ashbrook's South Side for three, and has stopped expecting the Alpha's office to fund her youth-sports petition. When Roman attends his quarterly hearing in person for the first time in eight years, the scent of one human woman in the third row stops his life. He carries her out of the hall in front of her brother, her business, and her city. In his pack, the claiming is celebrated. In her body, the bond does not take — because the bond cannot be forced. He has her. He does not have her consent. And the only thing he has ever truly wanted is the one thing he cannot make her give.

**Status:** First 10 chapters finalized. Bible complete.

## STORY 2 — *The Wolfless Princess*

**Logline:** A wolfless princess in a medieval all-werewolf realm is systematically erased from her own father's court by her stepmother — until a visiting Alpha catches her scent in the moonlit garden and his wolf knows her before he has seen her face.

**Blurb:** Princess Raina of Aldenmere should have been her father's heir. She is the senior princess by blood, the daughter of the late Luna Elaria, the child of a love-match that ended too early. She is also, at twenty-two, still without her wolf — and her stepmother has spent twelve years making sure no one in the realm forgets it. When Alpha Cassian of Tharnholt arrives for a week-long state visit and a possible alliance, Raina is kept from every receiving line, every feast, every run. Cassian, who has stopped expecting his mate, is being steered politely toward the polished stepsister. Then, on the third night, he walks the gardens alone. The wind shifts. He catches a scent that should not have been there at all — and the woman in the moonlight turns and runs before he can see her face.

**Status:** Bible complete. Chapters 1–5 drafted. Chapter 6 onward to be rewritten in a fresh chat.

## STORY 3 — *Crash Course*

**Logline:** A Formula One world champion bumps into a crèche teacher's car at a stoplight and is in love before she has finished swearing at him — but she doesn't know who he is, and she has no intention of finding out.

She gives him her insurance details and gets back in her car. He calls her the next afternoon. She thinks it is about the claim. It is not.

## STORY 5 — *The Painter and the Vampire*

**Logline:** A vampire who has hunted humanity for two centuries breaks into a woman's home meaning to drain her — and finds, instead, his bride painting in her attic by moonlight.

**Blurb:** 1950s America, in a city that has not learned yet how to catch a vampire. Vladimir Reszny has been killing his way across the country for two hundred years, since he gave up looking for the bride he had dreamed of all his life. Then the dreams come back, sharper and faster than before. He follows them to a city he has never visited and a house he means only to feed in. The woman in the attic is the woman he has been dreaming of for two centuries. She is painting the moon. She does not know he is there. He cannot leave her. He cannot tell her what he is. He can, however, learn everything about her — and remove the man who has just begun to court her, because nothing in this world has ever belonged to anyone the way she belongs to him. A two-thread narrative: the love story, and the homicide detectives on his trail.

**Logline:** A federal forensic accountant walks into the lobby of a shipping conglomerate to audit it for laundering — and the don who owns the building is in love before she reaches the elevator.

**Blurb:** Salvatore De Luca has run the De Luca family for eight years and has never met a problem he could not bury, bribe, or shoot. The threats are real — they are coming from his own house — and the evidence she is looking for is sitting in his desk drawer, and every day she comes closer to it, and every day he is more certain he will not be able to let her go when she finds it.

## DRAFTING WORKFLOW (current)

For each story, the workflow is:

1. **Bible** — world rules, character profiles, naming, guardrails, no-go list. Lives at `bible.md` inside that story's folder.
2. **Chapter drafts** — generated in batches of ~10 chapters, reviewed, finalized.
3. **Chapter log files** — the continuity reference system described below. Critical for handoff between LLMs across the 200-chapter arc.
4. **Audio-tagged narration script** — final ElevenLabs-ready format. Generated only after chapters are fully finalized. Lives at `chapters_audio.md`.

A complete handoff package for any story-in-progress consists of:
- This master catalog
- That story's `bible.md`
- That story's `summary.md` (compressed master summary — see below)
- That story's most recent `chapter_log_XXX-YYY.md` (full detail for the active batch)
- Any chapter drafts in progress

## CHAPTER LOG SYSTEM (FOR ALL LLMs WORKING ON ANY STORY IN THIS CATALOG)

**Read this section carefully before generating, editing, or maintaining any chapter log file in this project.**

### Purpose

The chapter log system is the continuity backbone of the 200-chapter arc. It exists because the bible cannot grow indefinitely (it would become unreadable) and because no LLM has the context window to remember 200 chapters of beat-by-beat detail. The system uses **hierarchical summarization** — keep recent content in full fidelity, compress older content aggressively, re-summarize at intervals so total size grows logarithmically rather than linearly.

### Two file types per story

1. **`chapter_log_XXX-YYY.md`** — detailed log of the most recent batch (typically 10 chapters). Always current, always ~3,000-4,000 words. Contains beat-by-beat summaries, POV notes, planted threads, first-introductions, continuity anchors. This is the file the next-chapter writer reads first.

2. **`summary.md`** — rolling compressed master summary of all chapters older than the current detailed batch. Grows slowly as older chapters age out of the detailed log and into the summary at progressively higher compression ratios. By chapter 200 this file should be no more than ~10,000-12,000 words total — fully usable as reference.

### When to compress (the batch-boundary rule)

Compression happens at each **batch boundary** — i.e., when the writer finalizes a new batch of chapters (chapters 10-19, then 20-29, etc.) and is ready to start the next batch.

Concretely: just before generating `chapter_log_020-029.md`, the LLM must:
1. Take the contents of the previous `chapter_log_010-019.md`.
2. Compress those chapters into the `summary.md` file according to the tier rules below.
3. Delete or archive the previous `chapter_log_010-019.md` (user choice — default: archive in a subfolder called `archive/`).
4. Begin the fresh `chapter_log_020-029.md`.

### Compression tiers (by chapter age)

When compressing, apply the following based on how many chapters back the entry is from the current writing position:

| Age (chapters back) | Detail level | Approximate length per chapter |
|---|---|---|
| Most recent batch (1–10 chapters back) | Full beat-by-beat | 200–400 words each (lives in `chapter_log_XXX-YYY.md`) |
| 11–30 chapters back | Summarized | 3–5 sentences each |
| 31–50 chapters back | Tightened | 1–2 sentences each |
| 51+ chapters back | Arc-collapsed | Group 10–20 chapters into a single 2–4 sentence arc description |

### What NEVER gets compressed away

The following must be preserved in full inside `summary.md` regardless of how old the source chapter is:

1. **Verbal commitments** made by any main character to another. (Example: Roman's "I will not take anything you do not give" type lines, once they appear on page, are inviolable.) These live in a dedicated section at the top of `summary.md` called **VERBAL COMMITMENTS — INVIOLABLE**.

2. **First-introductions of characters** — name, age, role, key physical/personality details. These live in a section called **CHARACTERS INTRODUCED (cumulative)**.

3. **Established sensory anchors** — scent profiles, eye colors, heights, key physical tells, distinctive habits. Live in **CONTINUITY ANCHORS (cumulative)**.

4. **Significant locations** introduced. Live in **LOCATIONS INTRODUCED (cumulative)**.

5. **Open series threads** — plot threads opened but not yet resolved. Live in **OPEN THREADS (current state)**. Threads that get resolved get moved to **RESOLVED THREADS (with chapter reference)**.

6. **Major plot events** — anything a future chapter must remember to maintain continuity. These get one line each in **MAJOR EVENTS TIMELINE**.

### Structure of `summary.md`

The file should always be organized in this order:

```
# SUMMARY — [Story Name or Story Number]

## VERBAL COMMITMENTS — INVIOLABLE
(Direct quotes of all on-page commitments made by main characters.)

## CHARACTERS INTRODUCED (cumulative)
(Name, role, key facts. Grows by addition only.)

## LOCATIONS INTRODUCED (cumulative)
(Place names and key facts.)

## CONTINUITY ANCHORS (cumulative)
(Scent profiles, physical tells, established sensory details.)

## OPEN THREADS (current state)
(Numbered list of unresolved plot threads.)

## RESOLVED THREADS (with chapter references)
(Threads from the open list that have been closed.)

## MAJOR EVENTS TIMELINE
(One line per significant event, chronologically.)

## CHAPTER SUMMARIES BY AGE TIER
### Recent (11–30 chapters back)
- Chapter NN: [3–5 sentences]
- ...

### Tightened (31–50 chapters back)
- Chapter NN: [1–2 sentences]
- ...

### Arc-collapsed (51+ chapters back)
- Chapters NN–MM: [2–4 sentence arc description]
- ...
```

### Handoff to a new LLM

When passing the project to a new LLM (new chat, new model, new contributor), provide:

1. This master catalog (`master_catalog.md`).
2. The story's `bible.md`.
3. The story's `summary.md` (if it exists — only stories past their first batch have one).
4. The story's most recent `chapter_log_XXX-YYY.md`.
5. The actual chapter prose files (`chapters.docx` or equivalent).

Instruct the receiving LLM:
> "Before writing any new chapter, read in this order: master catalog → bible → summary → recent chapter log. Do not contradict anything in any of those files. When you finalize the current batch of chapters, compress the recent chapter log into the summary per the rules in the master catalog, then begin a new chapter log file for the next batch."

### First-batch exception

The very first batch of any story does not yet have a `summary.md` file — there is nothing older to compress. The summary file is created at the *second* batch boundary, when the first batch's detailed log gets compressed into it.
