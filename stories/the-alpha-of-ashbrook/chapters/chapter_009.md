# Chapter 9

## Someone Was Clapping

She had time to think one clear thing as he lifted her: *this isn't real.*

Then everything was very loud.

Diego was up and lunging across the empty seat where she had been, his hand reaching for the Alpha's arm. One of the Gammas crossed the distance between the corner and the third row in a way Elena's eye could not follow and put her brother on the floor without seeming to hit him. The hall erupted. People stood. A chair fell. The pack woman two rows down was crying *oh, oh, oh,* and the human petitioners in the back were shouting and a sign came down and the Alpha was carrying Elena like she weighed nothing through the middle aisle toward the door.

She was not screaming. She noticed this about herself with a clinical kind of distance. She was very still in his arms. His chest was hot under her shoulder. He smelled like cedar and something darker she could not place, and his arm under her knees was solid as a beam, and his mouth was at her temple.

"Put me down," she said. Her voice was quiet. She did not recognize it. "Put me down. Alpha. Put me down."

He did not put her down.

"You're safe," he said. His voice was low and torn open. "You're safe. You're safe."

"Put me down."

The doors of the hearing hall were ahead. Marcus was at his shoulder still talking, fast, urgent — *Alpha, the press, the optics, Alpha at least let her walk* — and the Alpha was not listening, and the doors burst open in front of them and the cold October air hit Elena's face and there were people on the steps and a black car at the curb with the back door already open and a driver standing beside it, and the Alpha carried her down the steps without breaking stride.

Behind her, distantly, she heard Diego roar her name.

Behind her, distantly, she heard someone clap. A pack woman, somewhere in the crowd, was *clapping.*

The Alpha set her into the back seat of the car. He climbed in after her. He pulled the door shut.

The car was already moving.

She turned her face to the window and watched the hall slide past, smaller and smaller, and her brother on the steps, smaller and smaller, and she could not feel her hands.
