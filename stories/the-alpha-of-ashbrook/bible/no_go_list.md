# NO-GO LIST — STORY 1: THE ALPHA OF ASHBROOK

> Things the chapter-generation skill must never write into a chapter.
> Read-only after Step 3b. Consulted every chapter.

---

## Plot and character

1. **Do NOT have Elena fall for Roman quickly.** She does not. The pull is one-sided for at least the first 60 chapters and significantly beyond. She experiences the early arc as a violation, not a fairy tale. Her softening is gradual and earned across many chapters.

2. **Do NOT soften the claiming.** From Elena's perspective the events of chapters 1–9 are a kidnapping. The narrative does not minimise this. The pack culture that accepts public claiming does not erase the wrongness of it from Elena's POV.

3. **Do NOT make Roman a villain.** He is morally grey by human standards, fully sympathetic by werewolf standards. He genuinely believes he is doing the right thing for both of them. His commitments are real and he keeps them.

4. **Do NOT write Elena as a victim.** She is in a horrifying situation and she is the smartest, most resourceful person in any room. She will fight him with every tool she has. Her escape thread runs through Arc 15. She is not passive even when she appears compliant.

5. **Do NOT have the wolf speak aloud.** The wolf is internal, italicised, always inside Roman's head. It surfaces as short urgent intrusions, never as dialogue.

6. **Do NOT depict cartoon-villain anti-human discrimination.** No on-page slurs. No outright cruelty as the texture of every scene. The discrimination is structural, mundane, deniable — that is what makes it real. The bullying werewolf women in Arc 8 onward are sneering and cold; they are not slinging slurs.

7. **Do NOT use the word "senator."** Roman is Alpha. Always. American political vocabulary can describe the surrounding world (constituents, op-eds, the press pool, the opposition bloc) but the title is never "senator."

## Romance and heat

8. **Do NOT bring the bond before Arc 19.** Elena does not feel the bond until the bite in Arc 19. Do not have flashes of bond-feeling earlier. Do not have her experiencing his emotions before the bite. The bond gap is absolute until the consent moment.

9. **Do NOT have penetrative sex before Arc 19.** Tier 1 stops at the act. The line is firm. The slow-burn thread is the buildup; the bond night is the payoff.

10. **Do NOT bite Elena without her on-page consent.** The bite happens in Arc 19. Elena's spoken yes precedes the bite. This is the bible's central rule. The bond cannot be forced.

11. **Do NOT have Elena initiate physical escalation before Arc 7.** The first kiss is Roman-initiated (she kisses back). Major escalations from there are mutual or Roman-led until Arc 19, when Elena initiates the bond night by walking into his shower.

12. **Do NOT write the slow-burn touch scenes as identical events.** Each of the eleven big touch scenes is a different setting, a different emotional register, and a different physical escalation. No two are interchangeable.

13. **Do NOT have Roman use Alpha-voice compulsion on Elena.** Ever. He will not compel her. The bond's voluntariness is the central tension of the romance; compulsion would destroy that.

## Style and craft

14. **Do NOT reuse phrases across chapters** for recurring elements. Every chapter that touches his eyes shifting to gold, the pull, the lean of the wolf, her ponytail, her scent, the cheek-bite, the wrist tracing — every chapter freshens its language for those things.

15. **Do NOT psychoanalyse the characters in narration.** Elena's motives and Roman's interior life surface through action, observation, and dialogue. The narrator does not step back and explain *Elena was afraid because of her father's death and the systemic oppression of her people.* The narrator shows her clenched jaw, her short breath, the chalk on her palms.

16. **Do NOT use purple prose.** One precise image is worth three vague ones. No stacking adjectives. No metaphor pile-ups. The voice is observational and sharp, not lyrical.

17. **Do NOT use "she/he said angrily" or other adverb dialogue tags.** Use action to carry the emotion. Use plain *he said* and *she said* when a tag is needed.

18. **Do NOT close a chapter with all questions settled.** Every chapter ends on a hook — unresolved emotion, a question raised, a forward-pointing line, a detail that pulls to next week. Never a clean stop.

19. **Do NOT narrate the rule.** The chapter prose is fiction. It does not reference the bible, the locations.md, the skeleton, the heat tier, or any other system input. *Do not write: "this was the first of the slow-burn touch scenes."* The reader does not know there is a list.

## Locations

20. **Do NOT invent locations.** Use only locations in `locations.md`. If a chapter genuinely needs a location not in the file, that requires user action (manual edit). The chapter cannot proceed with a fabricated location.

21. **Do NOT teleport characters during active movement.** If a character is walking somewhere, their path must be consistent with adjacencies in `locations.md`. Travel times in the file are the reference. If a scene jumps from one place to another, the jump must be a clean scene-break (chapter break or paragraph break with time-skip), not a continuous walking sequence that crosses impossible distances.

## Politics

22. **Do NOT have humans win politically through violence.** Diego's first action is destruction of a small government building — successful, but symbolic. His second action is a documents heist — fails, by design. The bills that actually change things (Equal Residency Act) pass through political chess play. Violence is a real choice the characters make but the story does not validate it as the effective lever.

23. **Do NOT have Roman openly side with the resistance.** Roman is a reformist Alpha working within the system. He does not endorse Diego's cell. When Diego is caught he is appalled — at Elena, at the cell, at the situation. He gets Diego out off-page because Diego is Elena's brother and Diego is human enough to be treated badly in detention. Roman is not a revolutionary.

## Characters who must not appear (or appear only as referenced)

24. **Roman's deceased father:** referenced only. Never on page in flashback or dream.
25. **Helena's other family:** Helena has no other children beyond Roman and Cassia. She is not part of an extended pack-political dynasty. She is the dowager Vance — and that is the limit of her social position. The bible says nothing about her own siblings, and the chapter-generation skill should not invent them.
26. **Holloway's family:** Holloway is referenced as a powerful Alpha. He has a family (typical for his demographic) but they do not appear on page in this story.

## Things the story is not about

27. **Not about converting Elena to werewolf supremacy.** Elena does not come to accept the dystopia. She comes to choose Roman within it and to fight for change from inside. These are different things. The story is not propaganda for the system; the system is the antagonist alongside Holloway.
28. **Not about Roman being wrong about everything.** Roman is genuinely the best version of an Alpha in this world. His mistakes are the mistakes of someone who has been in power too long without seeing what power costs others. He learns. He does not become a different person.
29. **Not about pregnancy as the goal.** Elena's pregnancy is the final image but it is not the engine of the story. The bond is the romantic engine; reform is the political engine; the petition closing the loop is the structural engine. Pregnancy is a closing grace note.
