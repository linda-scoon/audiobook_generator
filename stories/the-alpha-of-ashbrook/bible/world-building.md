# WORLD-BUILDING — STORY 1: THE ALPHA OF ASHBROOK

> Setting deep-dive. Geography, politics in operation, culture, daily life.
> Read-only after Step 3b.

## 1. THE COUNTRY (UNNAMED)

This is a modern-day alternate-Earth dystopia. The country parallels the contemporary United States — territories that function like states, an Alpha Council that functions like the Senate, a mid-sized urban-and-rural mix, an English-speaking majority. Cars, phones, internet, social media all work as in our world.

The country has no formal name. Characters refer to "the territories" collectively or simply "the country." There is no federal layer above the pack territories. Each Alpha rules his pack lands absolutely; the Council exists for cross-territory matters only.

This country is **werewolf-majority (70/30)** — globally unusual. Most of the rest of the world is closer to 50/50 or human-majority. The dystopia is local.

## 2. ASHBROOK — THE CITY

Mid-sized, ~800,000 population. The story takes place primarily in and around Ashbrook, which sits within the Vance pack territory.

### Highland District (north)
Werewolf elite live here. Gated estates, hills, polished streets. The Vance Estate is in the Highland District. Cassia's penthouse is central Highland. Helena's dowager estate is just outside the city. The Wildwood Club sits at the outer edge of Highland with 200 acres of attached forest.

### Mixed middle (city centre)
Middle-class neighbourhoods, mixed werewolf and human populations. Downtown business district. Government buildings. Press headquarters. The Alpha Council Building complex is here. Roman's downtown office is here.

### South Side (south of Halford Avenue)
Human-majority working-class neighbourhoods. Cruz Community Fitness is on Hidalgo Avenue. Marisol's bakery is two doors west of the gym. The Cruz family home is several blocks away. Diego and Lucía's home is in another South Side residential block. The South Side Hearing Hall is on the corner of Hidalgo and Vega. Streets, businesses, daily life — visibly under-resourced compared to Highland.

### Halford Avenue
The boundary between Highland and South Side. Crossing it is a sensory event — air, smell, light all change. From the north, Highland is cut grass and stone. From the south, South Side is fryer oil, laundry, exhaust, river.

## 3. POLITICAL LIFE IN ACTION

The Alpha Council is the cross-territory governing body. Sessions are quarterly. Most business is dull inter-pack negotiation — trade agreements, infrastructure cost-sharing, dispute resolution between neighbouring packs.

**Bills:** Alphas can propose legislation that would apply across all territories. A bill must reach consensus among the Council Alphas to pass. There is no majority-vote system; consensus is the rule. In practice this means an Alpha can torpedo a bill by refusing to consent. The chess-play strategy (introducing a louder bill as a distraction so a quieter bill passes uncontested) works because Alphas focus their attention on what the press is covering.

**Within his own pack territory**, an Alpha's word is law. He needs no Council consent to set local policy. The bills in this story are cross-territory because the reforms require all territories to comply — a single Alpha lifting standards in his own pack only is welcome but doesn't fix the country.

**Press:** national press covers Council sessions, Alpha biographies, scandals. The Sentinel is the local Ashbrook paper. National outlets carry Roman's story after Arc 7. Foreign press exists and picks up significant stories from this country.

**Holloway** is the Alpha of a neighbouring pack territory. Hardline anti-human. Long-standing presence on the Council. Antagonist throughout.

## 4. PACK CULTURE

Pack life is communal in ways human life isn't. Pack gatherings are frequent — full moon arrangements, naming ceremonies, first-shift ceremonies, hunts. The Vance Estate is the venue for most Vance pack pack-wide events.

Werewolves run as wolves regularly — Cassia's circle runs at the Wildwood Club, the pack runs together at full moons, individuals run alone in private forests. Running is exercise, communion, and instinct.

**Shifting:** under 10 seconds for trained wolves. Clothes don't survive a shift. Werewolves think nothing of being naked post-shift; humans do. Elena's first encounters with post-shift nudity (Roman after catching her, Cassia after a Wildwood run) are jarring.

**Food:** rare and raw meat is standard. Cassia's circle at lunch will order steaks blue or raw. Pack gatherings feature whole roasted animals, fires, communal eating. Elena eats medium — she has to ask.

**Sensory life:** werewolves catch scent and intent from a room away. They know when someone is lying, angry, attracted, ill. Elena has none of this. She reads faces like a human. Werewolves around her sometimes forget she can't pick up what they pick up.

**Greetings:** werewolves often greet by scenting — a quick lean toward neck or wrist. Done to humans only with permission or in family context.

**Bonded mates:** in werewolf culture, an Alpha's mate carries enormous social weight. Once Elena is recognised as Roman's mate, even before the bond shares, she has standing — her insults are felt as insults to the Alpha, her favour is sought, her dismissal is feared. This is the source of Helena's leverage and Elena's growing power across the arc.

## 5. THE PACK SPLIT ON HUMAN MATES

Werewolves react to a human mate in three rough camps:

- **Adoring traditionalists.** A fated mate is a fated mate. They celebrate. They were the ones clapping on the hearing hall steps in Ch 9. They will throw the mating party in Arc 2.
- **Confused or cautious.** They don't oppose the mate but don't know how to behave around her. Most of pack society sits here at first.
- **Cold or hostile.** They see the human mate as a stain on the Alpha or the pack. They are not in the majority but they are loud. They are the source of the bullying Elena and Helena face.

The split is real. Roman doesn't try to suppress it. He lets the pack work it out and exerts authority only when it directly threatens Elena.

## 6. HUMAN COMMUNITY LIFE

The South Side is a tight community. Humans look out for humans through quiet community networks. They share information about which businesses won't serve them, which cops to avoid, which neighbourhoods to stay out of after dark.

Cruz Community Fitness is a hub — pre/post-natal fitness for human women, youth athletics programs, low-priced classes. Elena is locally known and respected.

**Marisol's bakery** is two doors west. Marisol is Catholic, traditional, late 50s. She does not trust werewolves and never has. She loves her daughter.

**Diego** is a contractor and a quiet community organiser. By Arc 9 he is running an active resistance cell.

**The activist culture** in the South Side has long been quiet. Petitions, community meetings, peaceful protests. Diego's cell represents an escalation — direct action. Most of the community does not know about the cell. Lucía knows.

## 7. ECONOMY AND CLASS

Roman is wealthy. The Vance Estate has 40 acres, 12 bedrooms, and staff who come in daily. He owns multiple cars, the lake house, the resort access. Cassia runs the pack commercial portfolio — significant family money.

Elena was, by South Side standards, doing well — owner of a successful gym, second-generation Mexican-American, college-educated. By Highland standards, she is poor. The class displacement she experiences is the gap between those two scales. She can navigate Highland materially (Roman provides) but the cultural fluency takes work.

## 8. THE PETITION AND THE EMPTY LOT

Cruz Community Fitness sits on Hidalgo Avenue. **Next door is an empty lot.** Elena has been petitioning the city for three years to convert the lot into a youth sports facility — proper playground, court, indoor space. The neighbourhood community centre next door closed two years ago due to funding cuts. The youth programs Elena runs out of the gym are oversubscribed.

Her petition has been denied three times. Each time Marcus Hale signed the denial on behalf of the Alpha's office. Roman never personally saw the petition until Ch 1 of the story.

**This is the petition that brought her to the hearing hall.** This is the lot that gets built into the sports ground in the final image of Ch 200.

## 10. PHYSICAL LAW

- Werewolves heal in hours from cuts, bruises, breaks. Severe injuries take days.
- Werewolves can run for hours as wolves without flagging. A wolf in pursuit catches a human in open ground every time.
- Werewolves can smell a known scent at hundreds of metres in good conditions, less in wind or rain.
- Werewolves hear conversations through walls.
- Werewolves cannot pick locks better than humans. Strength helps but technique matters.
- Werewolves catch lies by scent and pulse. Skilled humans can mask, but most don't.
- Phones, cars, computers all work normally. There is no magic. Werewolves are the only supernatural element in this world.

## 11. ROGUE STATUS — DAILY OPERATION

Rogue status is one of the chief instruments of human disadvantage. To live in a pack territory legally, a human must be registered with that pack. Rogue humans face periodic sweeps, detention, and deportation to a pack willing to receive them.

Rogue camps are referenced in this story but not shown on page. Diego's cell believes the camps exist and are worse than the state admits. Conditions are rumoured. Records are hidden inside the Council Building. Diego's heist target includes verification of rogue camp conditions among the broader injustice documentation.
