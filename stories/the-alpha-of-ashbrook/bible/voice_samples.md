## ROMAN VANCE — male lead, POV character

Roman's interiority is measured, observational, controlled. He notices the world before he names his own feelings. The wolf surfaces in italicised intrusions — short, urgent, animal. Roman the man calculates; the wolf wants. Sentence length varies. He is sensory but not lyrical.

**Sample 1 — a quiet morning observing:**

> The fog had not lifted from the pond. He drank his coffee black and watched a heron lift from the reeds and resettle further out. His phone buzzed once on the stone railing. Marcus's morning brief. He did not pick it up. The wolf was quiet. *Settled.* He had stopped expecting that to mean anything. The heron stretched its long throat at the water and stilled.

**Sample 2 — recognition or intense focus:**

> The wolf had gone still in him. Not absent — *attentive*, the way an animal becomes attentive when something small and good is moving through the grass. He set the pen down without looking at it. The clerk's tablet was making the small electric sound the clerks always made, and the room smelled like cheap fluorescent tubes and folding chairs, and somewhere underneath that was the thing he had been smelling without knowing he was smelling it. He looked at the third row.

**Sample 3 — controlled emotion, possessive register:**

> She was three feet from him and her pulse was at her throat and he could see it. He did not move. He let her finish the sentence she had begun. He let her say what she had come down the hall to say. *Mine. Mine and hers.* The wolf was not impatient. The wolf had already decided.

## ELENA CRUZ — female lead, POV character

Elena's interiority is sharp, alert, dry. She narrates surroundings before her own emotions. She clocks power in every room. Sensory specificity. Spanish phrases drift in when she is emotional. She is funny when she is not in danger and silent when she is.

**Sample 1 — daily observation, dry register:**

> The studio smelled like lemon cleaner and the lavender oil Tomás insisted on. She had told him a hundred times it was too much and he had told her a hundred times that pregnant women liked it. Anya, on her mat at the front, was holding the breath for ten with the kind of competitive face you took into a wrestling ring. Elena let her have it. She had three years of grant denials in the folder by her foot.

**Sample 2 — fear, going quiet:**

> She didn't say anything when the door closed. She heard the lock and she heard his footsteps on the stairs and she heard the housekeeper somewhere down the hall, humming. The window had a view of the lawn. The lawn went on a long way and then it went into trees and the trees went on a long way and then the trees stopped where the road stopped. She watched all of that. She did not move. She was not crying.

**Sample 3 — bilingual, emotional register:**

> Her hands were shaking. She made them stop. *Ya, ya, ya.* She pulled the elastic off her wrist and put her hair up and the elastic snapped and she pulled the second one off her wrist and put her hair up again. Diego was talking somewhere on the other side of the room and the room was very small. She didn't hear him. Mateo's little wooden lion was on the table next to her phone and she picked it up and put it down. *Madre de Dios.*

## DIEGO CRUZ — Elena's brother, dedicated POV across 10 chapters

Diego's interiority is plainer than Elena's, with shorter sentences and less ornament. He is a contractor by trade and a quiet community organiser by conviction. He thinks practically. He loves his family fiercely and tries not to show it. Spanish drifts in like Elena's but less often — Diego's Spanish is for prayer and for his son.

**Sample 1 — practical thought:**

> He sat in the truck for a minute before he went in. The light over the door was out again. He'd have to fix it. The lock on the back gate was rusting and Lucía had been telling him for two weeks. He needed to be home more. He needed to be home less. He needed his sister back.

**Sample 2 — anger held in:**

> The Alpha was waiting on the line. Diego could hear him breathing. He held the phone away from his face for a second and looked at the kitchen window and then put the phone back. *She is my sister. She has been my sister for thirty years. You have known her for nine days.* He did not say any of that. He said, "If you hurt her, Alpha. If you put one finger on her she has not wanted there. I will come for you."

**Sample 3 — love and fear together:**

> Mateo was asleep on the couch with the wooden lion in his fist. Lucía was at the kitchen table with her coffee cooling and her face in her hands. He stood in the doorway and looked at both of them. The lion's mane was painted yellow and the paint was wearing off. He had carved that lion in three evenings the year Mateo turned three. *Por nosotros, hijo. Por todos nosotros.*

## HELENA VANCE — Roman's mother, dedicated POV across 2 chapters

Helena's interiority is dignified, watchful, slowly hardening. She is old enough to remember when the world was smaller for her too. She measures every room she enters. Her growth across the book is from absorbing slights to refusing them.

**Sample 1 — observation in pack society:**

> The Alpha's table was set with the cream service. Helena noted it and did not comment. Three women she had known for twenty years were arranged around it as if they had been expecting her, which they had. The youngest of them was wearing the gold at her throat that meant a new mate and the eldest had the small dry mouth that meant she was about to say something she had been preparing all morning.

**Sample 2 — moment of decision:**

> The girl flinched and Helena saw it. The other woman saw it too and her mouth did the small thing it did when it was pleased with itself. Helena set her cup down. The clink it made on the saucer was louder than it should have been. She said, very politely, "I think we have decided."

## CASSIA VANCE — Roman's sister, dedicated POV across 2 chapters

Cassia's interiority is quick, businesslike, warmer than her brother's. She runs the pack commercial portfolio and she thinks in deals. She does not have his weight; she has his teeth. She loves her brother and is wary of his happiness because she has never seen him be this happy.

**Sample 1 — appraisal:**

> Elena was holding the cup with both hands the way she always did, as if she had to remind herself the cup was hers. Cassia watched her over the rim of her own. The Wildwood salads were absurd. Three kinds of pickled vegetable. The other women at the table were eating around them. Elena was eating them on principle. *Good*, Cassia thought. *Keep doing that.*

**Sample 2 — protective instinct:**

> Diego had asked twice. Cassia had not noticed the second time. The first time she had told him no — she did not know where Elena was — and her body had told her she was lying. Her body told her many things. The second time he asked, the bar around the corner had a light on it that meant the back door was open. She had wondered, then. She wondered now.

## ALPHA HOLLOWAY — antagonist, single POV chapter in Arc 9

Holloway's interiority is sour, sharp, a little brittle. He has been on the Council for decades. He thinks the country is what it is because it ought to be. He does not see himself as cruel; he sees himself as honest.

**Sample 1 — the antagonist alone:**

> Vance was up to something. He had been up to something for six months and Holloway could not put his finger on it. The boy was thirty-eight years old and he had never been a boy except that Holloway remembered him at twelve, at the funeral. The boy had cried then. The boy had not cried since. The bill on the floor was a piece of theatre. Holloway knew theatre when he saw it. He did not know what the play was about.

## NARRATIVE VOICE — third-person close conventions

These samples illustrate the close-third register the chapters use. Note:

- The narrator does not name emotions abstractly. *She was sad* is replaced with *she set her cup down very carefully and did not look up.*
- The narrator does not psychoanalyse. *He felt protective of her because of his own absent mate-bond* is replaced with *he did not look away from her.*
- Roman POV chapters allow italicised wolf intrusions. No other POV does.
- Sensory specificity is the texture. Every chapter has at least one observed detail that grounds the scene.
