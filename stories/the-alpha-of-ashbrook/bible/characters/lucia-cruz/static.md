## Identity
- **Full name:** Lucía Cruz
- **Title/Role:** wife of Diego Cruz. Mother of Mateo. Moral counterweight to Diego's activism. Voice of the family side of the resistance question.
- **Age at story start:** mid-thirties (33–35).

## Physicality
- Average height for the family — around 5'4".
- Strong, capable build.
- Long dark hair, often tied back.
- Warm brown skin, similar to the Cruz family colouring.
- Dark brown eyes.
- Hands that have done a lot of work.

## Family
- Married to Diego Cruz.
- Mother of Mateo Cruz, 6–7 years old.
- Extended family in the South Side neighbourhood.

## Background
- South Side native.
- Has worked variously over the years — admin work, retail, currently part-time at a community clinic.
- Raised Catholic. Less devout than Marisol but the faith is part of how she carries things.

## Personality
- Pragmatic. Tired. Principled.
- Loves Diego. Hates his activism for what it puts on Mateo.
- Not a coward — when she pushes back on Diego it is not from fear of consequences for herself but from refusal to lose her son to this country's prisons.
- Funny in private when she is not exhausted.

## Speech patterns
- Practical, direct.
- Bilingual; Spanish at home with Diego and Mateo, English otherwise.
- The recurring beat: *why do you bother?* This is her question to Diego across multiple scenes.

## Internal voice
- Does not get a dedicated POV chapter.
- Shown only through what she says and does in Diego's POV chapters.

## Position on the events of the book
- Furious about Elena being taken — Elena is her sister-in-law and she loves her.
- Resistant to Diego's escalation. Tries to talk him out of the cell work.
- When Diego is arrested in Arc 14, Lucía's world breaks.
- When Diego is released in Arc 16, she holds him and does not say *I told you so.* She has earned the right and does not use it.

## Habits
- Drinks coffee black.
- Goes to mass less often than Marisol but goes.
- Keeps the family's life running while Diego is doing what Diego does.
