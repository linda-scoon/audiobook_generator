# MARISOL CRUZ — STATIC BIBLE

> Permanent facts about Marisol Cruz.
> Read-only after Step 3b.

---

## Identity
- **Full name:** Marisol Cruz
- **Title/Role:** mother of Elena and Diego. Widow of Hector Cruz. Owner-operator of Cruz Bakery in the South Side. Appears on page only once in the main arc — when Roman visits in Arc 17.
- **Age at story start:** late 50s.

## Physicality
- Around 5'4". Soft build.
- Greying dark hair, usually pinned up under a bakery cap during the workday.
- Warm brown skin like her children.
- Dark brown eyes.
- Strong hands that have shaped a lot of bread.

## Family
- Widow of Hector Cruz, deceased seven years before story start (heart attack).
- Mother of Diego Cruz (34) and Elena Cruz (29).
- Grandmother of Mateo Cruz (6).

## Background
- South Side native.
- Came up with Hector. Built the bakery together over twenty-five years.
- Has run it alone since Hector's death.

## Personality
- Traditional. Catholic. Devout.
- Does not trust werewolves and never has.
- Loves her children fiercely.
- Quiet strength rather than loud opinions.

## Speech patterns
- Bilingual. Switches between Spanish and English depending on who she is talking to.
- Speaks slowly when emotional.
- Calls her children *mija* and *mijo* (Elena and Diego respectively).

## Position on the events of the book
- Will be told by Diego in the hours after Ch 9 that her daughter has been taken.
- Will be heartbroken, furious, and helpless in roughly that order.
- Will continue to run the bakery because there is nothing else she can do.
- Will receive periodic news of Elena over the months.
- Will meet Roman face-to-face in Arc 17. Dinner at the family home. She will not embrace him. She will not be openly hostile. She will watch him.

## Habits
- Wakes at 4am most mornings to start the day's baking.
- Goes to mass on Sundays.
- Keeps a wooden cross on the wall above the bakery counter.

## End of static file
