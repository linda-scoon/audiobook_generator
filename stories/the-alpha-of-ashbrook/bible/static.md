## 1. WORLD RULES — WEREWOLF BIOLOGY

- Werewolves are physically superior to humans: stronger, faster, more durable, enhanced scent and hearing. They heal from injury in hours. Lifespan is normal-human range (70s–90s). They retain more vigour into old age than humans but the body still ages.
- Females have heat cycles, males have ruts. Together these produce high fertility. Population is roughly 70% werewolf / 30% human in this country (the rest of the world is more balanced).
- Werewolves shift between human form and full-wolf form. **No hybrid/wolfman form exists.** They are either fully human or fully wolf. A shift takes under 10 seconds for trained wolves. Clothes do not survive a shift.
- Each werewolf has an internal "wolf" — a second consciousness, animal-instinct-driven, that shares the body. The human and the wolf can disagree. The wolf is rendered in italics in narration when it surfaces. **The wolf never speaks aloud.** It speaks inside.
- Alphas are the strongest werewolves. They can compel obedience from lower-ranked wolves via "the Alpha voice" — a rarely-used command that lower wolves cannot disobey.

## 2. THE MATE BOND — CORE MECHANIC

- Every werewolf is fated to one mate per lifetime. Called the true mate.
- Recognition is **instant and inescapable**. Triggered by scent (primary), then sight, sometimes voice.
- The wolf identifies the mate before the human side does. There is an internal moment of certainty — *MATE.* — that cannot be reasoned away.
- Symptoms in the werewolf on recognition: explosive protectiveness, possessiveness, inability to focus on anything else, physical pull toward the mate, irrational rage at any threat (real or perceived).
- A werewolf CAN refuse the bond. It causes severe physical and psychological damage. Refusal is socially scandalous and extremely rare.
- Most werewolves find their mate between ages 18–25. Finding them later is emotional pain only — loneliness, longing, a sense that something essential is missing. No physical illness.
- **Human mates exist.** The human IS the werewolf's true mate at the biological level — same as a werewolf mate would be. The difference is sensory, not relational. Humans lack the supernatural sense to feel the bond from their side until it is shared.
- **Bond-sharing:** the werewolf can transfer the bond to a human mate by biting. Once shared, the human feels the bond identically — full pull, full recognition, full certainty. Once shared, it cannot be undone.
- **Consent is absolute.** The bond cannot be forced. A werewolf physically cannot impose the bond on a human who has not accepted it — it simply does not take. This is true even after a public claiming, even after kidnapping, even after marriage. **The act of taking her does not transfer the bond. She has to say yes, knowingly, in the moment.**
- **Cultural norm — critical:** when an Alpha publicly recognises his mate, claiming her on the spot is considered acceptable and even expected among werewolves. The legal framework favours the werewolf: mating claims are legally recognised and effectively override the mate's consent in werewolf-controlled jurisdictions. Human civil rights organisations protest this. Nothing changes locally.

## 3. POLITICAL STRUCTURE

- The country is composed of **packs**. Each pack holds a **territory** — equivalent of a state. Pack lands span entire regions.
- **Each pack territory is one state.** There is no federal government above the territories. No president, no constitution, no centralising executive.
- An Alpha rules his territory absolutely — government, judiciary, military head within his pack lands.
- For cross-territory matters (trade, treaties, war, disputes, large-scale infrastructure), Alphas convene as the **Alpha Council**. The Council works by consensus, not majority vote. No permanent leader. Sessions are quarterly and on emergency call.
- **Naming, strict:**
  - The title is **Alpha**. Never "senator." The word "senator" does not exist in this world.
  - **Beta** is the Alpha's second. **Gamma** is the enforcer rank.
  - **Territory** is the formal word for a pack's land. "Vance territory."
  - **Pack lands** is the everyday phrase. "Vance pack lands," "Holloway pack lands."
- This world has always been this way. There is no remembered history of human rule. Humans alive today were born under pack law and know no other system locally.

## 4. INTERNATIONAL DIMENSION

- The dystopia is local to this country. The rest of the world has more balanced werewolf-to-human ratios. Werewolves abroad are integrated, not dominant.
- An international human-rights body exists (UN-equivalent for this world). It watches the local situation but has limited enforcement power.
- Foreign press picks up significant stories from this country.
- Elena's planned escape destination is abroad. Diego's stolen records would have been leaked to international observers. Roman, as he reforms, is aware international pressure is a real lever.

## 5. PACK MEMBERSHIP, MOVEMENT, AND ROGUES

- **Pack membership is mandatory** to live in a territory. Every resident — werewolf or human — is formally registered with the pack whose lands they live in. Registration is hereditary by default.
- **You cannot freely move between territories.** Crossing pack lands to live requires the receiving Alpha's permission. Visiting is permitted with documentation.
- **Rogues:** an unregistered resident is a rogue. The word applies to werewolves and humans equally. Rogues face detention, deportation, or worse if they resist.
- **Rogues are mostly human.** The system was built to control werewolf movement but ends up bearing down hardest on humans.

## 6. PACK HIERARCHY

- Alpha → Beta (second) → Gammas (enforcers) → general pack members.
- The Alpha's word is law within his territory. He can override any subordinate decision.
- The Beta runs day-to-day operations and is the Alpha's most-trusted advisor.
- Gammas serve as enforcement, security, and military.
- General pack members include all registered residents (werewolf and human). Humans cannot hold Alpha, Beta, or Gamma rank.

## 7. HUMAN–WEREWOLF RELATIONS (DYSTOPIAN TEXTURE)

- Humans have formal legal rights but are second-class in everyday practice.
- Werewolves get priority access to housing, business loans, medical care, government services.
- Mixed neighbourhoods exist; human-majority neighbourhoods are poorer, more under-resourced, more heavily policed.
- Human-only businesses face higher tax burdens and harder permitting.
- No overt segregation; the unwritten rules make daily life harder.
- Inter-species relationships happen but are stigmatised in werewolf circles UNLESS the human is a recognised mate. Then they are celebrated.
- **Concrete examples of structural discrimination:**
  - Pregnant human women receive lower-priority maternity care.
  - Public schools in human-majority neighbourhoods are visibly underfunded.
  - Police forces are disproportionately werewolf; over-policing of human areas.
  - Public funding requests from human community leaders are routinely denied.
  - Werewolves can use phrases like "your kind" toward humans without social consequence.
  - Werewolves walk into human-run businesses and expect priority service.

## 8. CORE CHARACTER PERMANENT FACTS

### Roman Vance — Alpha of the Vance Pack

- Age 38 at story start.
- 6'4", broad-shouldered, dense muscle. Built like he uses it.
- Dark brown hair, short, slight wave. Olive skin. Square jaw. Faint scar through left eyebrow (fight, age 24).
- **Eyes:** pale grey. Shift to gold when the wolf is close to the surface.
- **Wolf form:** massive grey wolf, near-black along the spine, gold eyes. Larger than an ordinary wolf — Alphas always are.
- **Family:** father deceased (natural causes, 10 yrs before story start). Mother Helena Vance alive — lives at her own dowager estate. One younger sister, Cassia Vance, 32, unmated.
- **Position:** Alpha for 12 years. Won by formal challenge at 26. The Vance territory includes the city of Ashbrook and surrounding counties. Holds a seat on the Alpha Council.
- **Speech:** short sentences when commanding. Longer when persuading. Rarely raises his voice — the lower it gets, the more dangerous. Uses possessive language: my pack, my territory, mine. Doesn't curse often. When he does, it lands.
- **Habits:** black coffee, no sugar. Whiskey neat, bourbon not Scotch. Tailored dark suits for public; jeans and dark t-shirts at home. Cooks his own breakfast when he can.
- **Physical tells:** pinches the bridge of his nose when controlling temper. Traces wrist → pulse → nape on Elena when calming his wolf (RESERVE for high-emotion moments). Eyes flash gold when wolf is near the surface.

### Elena Cruz — Owner of Cruz Community Fitness

- Age 29 at story start.
- 5'6", athletic build, strong (she trains), not lean-model thin.
- Warm brown skin (second-generation Mexican-American). Dark brown almost-black hair, usually in a low ponytail. Dark brown eyes. Hands strong, sometimes chalk-chapped.
- Small scar above right knee (childhood bike accident).
- **Family:** father Hector Cruz deceased (heart attack when Elena was 22, former PE teacher). Mother Marisol Cruz runs a small Mexican bakery in the same neighbourhood. Older brother Diego Cruz, 34, contractor.
- **Background:** born and raised in Ashbrook's South Side. Kinesiology + business degree, state university. Opened Cruz Community Fitness at 26.
- **Speech:** brisk, gets to the point. Bilingual — Spanish with mother and brother, English otherwise. Spanish phrases drift in when emotional. Curses freely when stressed or angry. Formal and precise when making a case to authority. When frightened or overwhelmed: goes very quiet.
- **Habits:** too much coffee, black or with cinnamon. Owns one nice dress — a deep green wrap dress, gift from mother. Reads paperback thrillers in bed to wind down.
- **Physical tells:** bites the inside of her cheek when thinking or controlling anger (RESERVE). Rotating cheap hair ties on her left wrist. Goes silent under real fear — no tears, no shaking, just stillness.
