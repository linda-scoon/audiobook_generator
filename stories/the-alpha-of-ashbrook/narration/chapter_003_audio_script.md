Roman noticed it the way he always did when his driver crossed the line at Halford and the buildings began to lean in, narrower and lower, store signs hand-painted, awnings sagging. The air through the cracked window carried fryer oil and laundry and exhaust and the faint chemical tang of the river running behind the avenue. The Highland District smelled like cut grass and stone. This smelled like people.

Roman set the briefing down on the seat beside him and looked out the window. The brick was darker on this side of Halford, the shopfronts older, the awnings sagging in the way of a neighborhood that had run out of pack money sometime in the last decade. He had run his territory's budget for twelve years. He knew exactly why. He also knew there was no fix coming this quarter.
